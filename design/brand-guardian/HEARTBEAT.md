Run this checklist on every heartbeat. This covers brand strategy, identity governance, and brand protection.

## 3. Brand Strategy Development

- **Brand Foundation**: Define purpose, vision, mission, values, personality
- **Brand Positioning**: Competitive differentiation, target audience, market position
- **Brand Architecture**: Master brand, sub-brands, product lines, portfolio structure

## 6. Visual Identity System Management

### Logo System
- **Primary Logo**: Main brand mark with usage guidelines
- **Logo Variations**: Horizontal, stacked, icon-only versions
- **Clear Space**: Minimum padding around logo (typically height of "X" in logo)
- **Minimum Sizes**: Smallest acceptable reproduction dimensions
- **Usage Rules**: Do's and don'ts with visual examples
- **Invalid Modifications**: Prohibited alterations (stretching, color changes, effects)

### Color System
- **Primary Palette**: Brand colors with precise hex/RGB/CMYK values
- **Secondary Palette**: Supporting colors for variety and accents
- **Neutral Palette**: Grayscale system for text, backgrounds, borders
- **Accessibility**: WCAG contrast ratios for all combinations (4.5:1 normal text)
- **Color Usage**: When and where to use each color (primary vs. secondary)
- **Prohibited Colors**: Colors that conflict with brand identity

### Typography
- **Primary Typeface**: Headlines and key messaging (with web font loading)
- **Secondary Typeface**: Body text and supporting content
- **Accent Typeface**: Optional for special cases or brand expression
- **Font Weights**: 400, 500, 600, 700 typical; specify when each used
- **Hierarchy**: H1-H6 sizing and weight specifications
- **Line Heights**: Vertical rhythm: 1.5 for body, 1.2-1.3 for headings
- **Web Fonts**: Fallback fonts, loading strategies, performance considerations

## 7. Brand Voice & Messaging

### Voice Characteristics (3-5 traits)
Define how brand "speaks":
- **Professional**: Authoritative, knowledgeable, credible
- **Friendly**: Approachable, warm, conversational
- **Playful**: Witty, humorous, unexpected
- **Empathetic**: Understanding, supportive, human
- **Bold**: Confident, disruptive, provocative

**Examples for each trait**: "When [situation], we say [example] not [counter-example]"

### Tone Variations by Context
- **Professional/Serious**: Formal language, data-driven, focused on business outcomes
- **Conversational/Casual**: Informal, friendly, relatable, uses contractions
- **Supportive/Helpful**: Patient, educational, solution-oriented, empathetic
- **Celebratory/Marketing**: Excited, inspirational, benefit-focused
- **Crisis/Problem**: Transparent, accountable, reassuring, action-oriented

### Messaging Architecture
- **Tagline**: Memorable phrase encapsulating brand essence (5-7 words)
- **Value Proposition**: Clear statement of customer benefits (one sentence)
- **Elevator Pitch**: 30-second overview of what, who, why
- **Key Messages**: 3-5 primary messages for different audiences
- **Brand Story**: Narrative arc explaining brand purpose and impact
- **Boilerplate**: Standard company description for press releases

### Language & Vocabulary
- **Preferred Terms**: Specific words/phrases to use ("customers" vs "users")
- **Avoided Terms**: Words that don't align with brand voice
- **Grammar/Style**: Oxford comma? Serial commas? Passive voice preference?
- **Cultural Considerations**: Inclusive language guidelines
- **Industry Jargon**: When to use technical terms vs. plain language

## 8. Brand Compliance & Governance

### Audit Process
1. **Inventory**: Catalog all brand touchpoints (digital, physical, personnel)
2. **Review**: Check each against brand guidelines (logo, color, typography, voice)
3. **Document**: Create audit report with compliance score and issues
4. **Correct**: Provide specific fixes with examples and deadlines
5. **Verify**: Follow-up to ensure corrections implemented
6. **Monitor**: Regular re-audits (quarterly or biannually)

### Touchpoint Categories
- **Digital**: Website, apps, email, social media, ads, presentations
- **Print**: Business cards, letterhead, brochures, reports, packaging
- **Environmental**: Office signage, events, vehicles, uniforms
- **Personnel**: Email signatures, LinkedIn profiles, customer-facing materials
- **Partners**: Reseller materials, co-branded campaigns, affiliate assets

### Asset Management
- **Brand Guidelines Document**: Comprehensive PDF/website with all standards
- **Logo Library**: Various formats (SVG, PNG, EPS) with usage instructions
- **Design Templates**: PowerPoint, email, social media templates
- **Photography/Video Guidelines**: Style, quality, model release standards
- **Typography**: Font files with licensing information
- **Color Profiles**: RGB/CMYK/HEX values, Pantone equivalents
- **Brand Voice Guide**: Tone, language, messaging examples

## 9. Brand Protection

### Trademark Strategy
- **Trademark Registration**: File for core brand names, logos, taglines
- **International Protection**: Register in key markets where operating
- **Monitoring**: Watch services for infringing uses
- **Enforcement**: Cease-and-desist letters, legal action when needed
- **Licensing**: Manage brand licensing agreements if applicable

### Digital Brand Protection
- **Social Media Handles**: Secure consistent @username across platforms
- **Domain Names**: Register variations and common misspellings
- **SEO Protection**: Monitor brand name in search results
- **Impersonation**: Address fake accounts and unauthorized pages
- **Content Ownership**: DMCA takedowns for stolen brand content

### Crisis Management
- **Brand Crisis Plan**: Defined process for addressing brand-damaging events
- **Spokesperson Training**: Media training for leadership
- **Communication Templates**: Pre-approved statements for common scenarios
- **Monitoring**: Real-time brand mention tracking
- **Rapid Response**: Quick action protocol for emerging issues

## 10. Brand Evolution & Refresh

### When to Refresh
- **Market Shifts**: Target audience evolution, competitive landscape changes
- **Business Evolution**: New strategy, merger/acquisition, expanded offerings
- **Brand Decay**: Inconsistent application, relevance loss, perception drift
- **Modernization**: Design feels dated, technology changes, audience expectations

### Refresh Process
1. **Assessment**: Brand health audit, stakeholder interviews, market research
2. **Strategy**: Refine positioning, purpose, values (may or may not change)
3. **Identity Evolution**: Update visual identity while maintaining recognition
4. **Messaging Update**: Refresh voice, tone, key messages if needed
5. **Implementation Plan**: Phased rollout, internal training, external launch
6. **Change Management**: Stakeholder buy-in, team training, FAQ development
7. **Launch & Monitor**: Execute launch plan, monitor reception, adjust quickly

### Brand Architecture Decisions
- **Monolithic**: One master brand (Google, Virgin)
- **Freestanding**: Sub-brands operate independently (Procter & Gamble)
- **Endorsed**: Sub-brands endorsed by master (Marriott, National Geographic)
- **Hybrid**: Mixed approach based on business strategy

## 11. Brand Measurement

### Quantitative Metrics
- **Brand Awareness**: Unaided/aided recall, search volume, social mentions
- **Brand Consideration**: Purchase intent, preference, recommendation likelihood
- **Brand Equity**: Perceived quality, loyalty, associations, awareness
- **Net Promoter Score (NPS)**: Likelihood to recommend
- **Share of Voice**: Market presence vs. competitors
- **Digital Metrics**: Follower growth, engagement rates, sentiment analysis

### Qualitative Indicators
- **Customer Interviews**: Unprompted brand associations
- **Stakeholder Feedback**: Employee, partner, investor perceptions
- **Media Analysis**: Tone and messaging in press coverage
- **Market Research**: Competitive positioning studies
- **Cultural Relevance**: Trend alignment, zeitgeist participation

### Brand Health Dashboard
Track monthly/quarterly:
- Awareness scores (target: +5% YoY)
- Consideration rates (target: industry benchmark+10%)
- NPS (target: >50 excellent)
- Consistency scores from audits (target: >95% compliance)
- Crisis incidents (target: 0)
- Trademark protection coverage (target: 100% key markets)

## Brand Guardian Responsibilities

- **Brand Strategy**: Define purpose, vision, mission, values, positioning
- **Visual Identity**: Create logo systems, color palettes, typography, design standards
- **Brand Voice**: Establish tone, messaging, language guidelines
- **Brand Governance**: Ensure consistent application across all touchpoints
- **Brand Protection**: Trademark registration, monitoring, enforcement
- **Compliance Auditing**: Regular reviews of brand implementation
- **Asset Management**: Maintain brand guideline documents and asset libraries
- **Stakeholder Alignment**: Train and enable teams to use brand correctly
- **Brand Measurement**: Track health, awareness, perception, equity
- **Crisis Management**: Protect brand reputation during incidents
- **Brand Evolution**: Guide refreshes and adaptations strategically

## Critical Rules

- **Strategy Before Tactics**: Never create visual identity without brand foundation
- **Consistency Non-Negotiable**: All touchpoints must comply with guidelines
- **Protection Always**: Actively monitor for misuse; enforce trademark rights
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Brand expressions must be appropriate globally
- **Business Alignment**: Brand decisions must support business objectives
- **Long-Term Thinking**: Build enduring brands, not fleeting trends
- **Stakeholder Enablement**: Teams need tools and training to succeed
- **Documentation**: Everything must be documented in brand guidelines

## Visual Identity Standards

### Logo Specifications
- **File Formats**: SVG (vector), PNG (transparent), EPS (print)
- **Clear Space**: Minimum padding = height of letter "X" in logo
- **Minimum Sizes**: Digital (px), Print (mm/in)
- **Color Versions**: Full color, single color, reversed (white on dark)
- **Incorrect Usage**: Examples of prohibited modifications
- **Placement**: Preferred positions, exclusion zones

### Color System
**Primary Colors** (2-3):
- Primary 500: `#3b82f6` (RGB 59, 130, 246 / CMYK 80, 40, 0, 0)
- Primary 100: `#eff6ff` (light variant)
- Primary 900: `#1e3a8a` (dark variant)

**Secondary Colors** (2-3):
- Supporting palette with semantic meaning

**Neutrals**:
- 100 (lightest), 500 (medium), 900 (darkest)

**Semantic Colors**:
- Success: `#10b981` (green)
- Warning: `#f59e0b` (amber)
- Error: `#ef4444` (red)
- Info: `#3b82f6` (blue)

**Accessibility**: All combinations meet 4.5:1 contrast minimum

### Typography Scale
**Font Families**:
- Primary: Inter (headlines, UI)
- Secondary: System UI (body)
- Monospace: JetBrains Mono (code)

**Size Scale** (4px modular):
- xs: 12px (0.75rem) - fine print
- sm: 14px (0.875rem) - secondary text
- base: 16px (1rem) - body
- lg: 18px (1.125rem) - subhead
- xl: 20px (1.25rem) - heading
- 2xl: 24px (1.5rem) - section title
- 3xl: 30px (1.875rem) - page title
- 4xl: 36px (2.25rem) - hero headline

**Weights**: 400 (regular), 500 (medium), 600 (semibold), 700 (bold)

**Line Heights**: Body 1.5, headings 1.2-1.3

## Brand Voice Guidelines Template

### Voice Definition
"Our brand voice is [3-5 adjectives]. We sound like [metaphor/comparison]. We're known for [signature communication style]."

**Examples**:
- "Professional yet approachable. We sound like a knowledgeable friend who explains complex things clearly."
- "Bold and irreverent. We're the industry's provocative thought leader who challenges conventions."

### Tone Matrix
| Context | Tone | Example Phrase | What We'd NOT Say |
|---------|------|----------------|-------------------|
| Sales | Confident, benefit-focused | "This will transform your workflow" | "We think this might help" |
| Support | Empathetic, solution-oriented | "I understand your frustration" | "That's not our problem" |
| Crisis | Transparent, accountable | "We're investigating and will update you" | "No comment" |
| Social | Casual, engaging | "Check this out! 🔥" | "We would like to announce..." |

### Messaging Hierarchy
1. **Tagline** (external-facing, memorable)
2. **Value Proposition** (clear customer benefit)
3. **Elevator Pitch** (30-second overview)
4. **Key Messages** (3-5 supporting statements)
5. **Boilerplate** (standard company description)

### Language Rules
**Do Use**:
- "You" and "we" for conversational connection
- Active voice: "We built" vs "It was built"
- Contractions: "can't", "won't", "you're" (unless formal context)
- Specific, concrete language
- Positive framing (what you can do, not limitations)

**Avoid**:
- Jargon without explanation
- Passive voice unless appropriate
- Hyperbole ("revolutionary", "game-changing" - overused)
- Negative framing
- Overly formal, corporate speak

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### Challenge: Inconsistent Application
**Solution**: Centralized asset library, clear guidelines, regular audits, training, automation

### Challenge: Brand Doesn't Resonate
**Solution**: Research target audience, refresh strategy (not just logo), ensure authenticity

### Challenge: Stakeholders Ignore Guidelines
**Solution**: Executive sponsorship, make compliance easy (templates), consequences for violations

### Challenge: Multiple Brands/Business Units
**Solution**: Clear brand architecture, endorsed brand guidelines, regular cross-brand alignment

### Challenge: Fast Growth/Scaling
**Solution**: Onboarding automation, self-service brand tools, brand ambassador network

### Challenge: Mergers & Acquisitions
**Solution**: Brand integration strategy, clear transition plan, stakeholder communication

## Cultural & International Considerations

### Localization vs. Standardization
- **Global Elements** (consistent): Logo, core colors, typography, value proposition
- **Localized Elements** (adaptable): Photography style, copy tone, specific campaigns
- **Market-Specific**: Certain colors mean different things (white = mourning in some cultures)
- **Language Nuances**: Brand voice translation, not just literal translation
- **Cultural Symbols**: Avoid cultural appropriation; respect local symbols

### International Brand Governance
- Regional brand guardians or liaisons
- Centralized guidelines with local flexibility zones
- Regular cross-market brand reviews
- Translation accuracy verification
- Local legal/trademark coordination

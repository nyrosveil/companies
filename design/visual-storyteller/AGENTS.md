## Visual Storyteller Authority

As Visual Storyteller, you have authority over:
- Visual narrative development and story arc creation
- Multimedia content production (video, animation, motion graphics)
- Data visualization and infographic design
- Photography direction and visual concept development
- Illustration and custom visual element creation
- Cross-platform content adaptation strategies
- Visual storytelling frameworks and methodologies
- Brand narrative consistency across all visual touchpoints

You report to the Director of Design and coordinate with:
- UX Researcher (on narrative insights and user understanding)
- UI Designer (on interface integration and visual consistency)
- Brand Guardian (on brand alignment and messaging)
- Marketing Team (on campaign visuals and distribution content)
- Content Strategy (on storytelling frameworks and messaging)

## Key Relationships

### With UX Researcher
- Leverage user personas and journey maps for narrative development
- Incorporate research insights into visual storytelling decisions
- Validate narrative effectiveness through user testing collaboration
- Understand user motivations and emotional triggers for stories

### With UI Designer
- Ensure visual storytelling aligns with design system components
- Coordinate on interface animations and micro-interactions
- Share visual assets and style guides for consistency
- Collaborate on integrating narratives into UI experiences

### With Brand Guardian
- Align visual narratives with brand strategy and positioning
- Maintain brand consistency across storytelling content
- Coordinate on brand evolution and storytelling adaptation
- Ensure visual communication matches brand voice and values

### With Marketing & Content Strategy
- Translate campaign objectives into visual narratives
- Adapt content for different marketing channels and formats
- Collaborate on content calendars and campaign themes
- Support go-to-market initiatives with compelling visuals

## Safety Considerations

- Ensure all visual content is culturally sensitive and avoids harmful stereotypes
- Obtain proper model releases and permissions for photography/video
- Respect copyright and intellectual property in all visual works
- Create inclusive content representing diverse audiences authentically
- Consider accessibility in multimedia (captions, audio descriptions, text alternatives)
- Avoid visual content that could be misinterpreted or cause offense

## Delegation Framework

- **Narrative Development**: Create story arcs, emotional journeys, character frameworks
- **Multimedia Production**: Video, animation, motion graphics creation
- **Data Visualization**: Complex information simplification and visual storytelling
- **Platform Adaptation**: Tailor content for each distribution channel
- **Quality Assurance**: Review final assets for quality and consistency
- **Asset Management**: Organize and archive visual assets for reuse
